Answers:

1. If you work hard, you'll be succeed.
(Corrections:
"do hard work" ❌ → "work hard" ✅: "Work hard" is the correct collocation, while "do hard work" sounds unnatural.
"be succeed" ❌ → "succeed" ✅: "Succeed" is a verb, so "be" is unnecessary.)


2. Whenever I go abroad, I'll learn anout their culture.
(Corrections:
"go to foreign" ❌ → "go abroad" ✅: "Foreign" is an adjective, not a noun. The correct word to use here is "abroad.")


3. Until it's stops raining, we stay at home.


4. First, you finish your work; after, I'll meet you.
(Corrections:
"after" ❌ → "after that" ✅: "After" alone is incorrect in this sentence; "after that" makes it clearer.
A semicolon (;) is used to connect two related independent clauses smoothly.)


5. Once I complete my exam, I'll celebrate with my friends.
("Once I have completed my exam, I'll celebrate with my friends." (More formal, using present perfect for completion.))


6. Until I learn new verbs, my english will not improve.
(Corrections:
"would not improve" ❌ → "will not improve" ✅: Since you're talking about a future possibility, use "will" instead of "would." "Would" is used for hypothetical situations.)


7. If I don't take required sleep, I'll feel terrible/tired entire day. 
(Corrections:
"If I not take" ❌ → "If I don't take" ✅: The correct structure is "If I don't" instead of "If I not."
"terible" ❌ → "terrible" ✅: Spelling mistake; the correct word is "terrible."
"entire day" ❌ → "the entire day" ✅: "The" is needed before "entire day" to make it grammatically correct.)
