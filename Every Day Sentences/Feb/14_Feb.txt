Answers:

1. Nowadays, he is working on his new project.


2. I think I couldn't understand my mistake.
2. I think I was not able to understand my mistake.


3. If he arrives on time, we'll go to watch a movie.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "go for watch" ❌ → "go to watch" ✅
"Go for" is used for nouns (e.g., "go for a walk"), but for verbs like "watch," we use "go to watch" instead.
2️⃣ "movie" ❌ → "a movie" ✅
In English, singular countable nouns need an article (a/an/the), so "a movie" is correct.)


4. You must study regularly otherwise you will not be able to pass the exam.
4. You must study regularly or you will not be able to pass the exam.


5. When I was a child, I played in the garden every day.
(Correction:
"was playing" ❌ → "played" ✅
Since this is a past habit, we use simple past ("played") instead of past continuous ("was playing").)


6. If you want to succeed, you must do hard work.
If you want to succeed, you must do work hard. (more natural)


7. He doesn't have the courage to ask her a question.


8. Yesterday, we only played football for three hours.
8. We played football for only three hours yesterday.


9. If he were here, he would definitely help you.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "If he was here" ❌ → "If he were here" ✅
For unreal or hypothetical situations in the present or future, we use "were" instead of "was" (this is known as the second conditional).
2️⃣ "will" ❌ → "would" ✅
In the second conditional, we use "would" instead of "will" to show hypothetical outcomes.
3️⃣ "definately" ❌ → "definitely" ✅
"Definitely" is the correct spelling.)


10. I have been waiting for you for last one hour.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "I was waiting" ❌ → "I have been waiting" ✅
The present perfect continuous tense (have been waiting) is used here because you're referring to an action that started in the past and continues to the present.
2️⃣ "for last one hour" ❌ → "for the last hour" ✅
Use "the last hour" instead of "last one hour" to specify a particular time period.)

Answers: 

11. They recently bought a new car.


12. If you want to top next year, start studying from now onwards.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "do top" ❌ → "top" ✅
"Top" is a verb here, so we don't need "do." The correct phrase is "want to top next year."
2️⃣ "start study" ❌ → "start studying" ✅
"Start" should be followed by a gerund (-ing form), so "start studying" is correct.
3️⃣ "onwords" ❌ → "onwards" ✅
The correct spelling is "onwards," not "onwords.")


13. I wished I could have arrived early.
13. I wish I could have gotten here a little earlier.


14. When I complete my college, I will go abroad to study.


15. When I was talking on phone, light .
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "on phone" ❌ → "on the phone" ✅
We always say "on the phone" (with "the") when referring to a phone conversation.
2️⃣ "light was turned on and off happened" ❌ → "the light kept turning on and off" ✅
"Was turned on and off happened" is incorrect. Instead, "kept turning on and off" better describes the continuous action.)


16. If you have any doubt, you can talk to me anytime. 

17. My sister has never been to the beach.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "never ever gotten" ❌ → "has never been" ✅
The correct tense for life experiences is "has never been" (present perfect).
"Never ever" sounds too informal and unnecessary here.
2️⃣ "beach" ❌ → "the beach" ✅
We usually say "the beach" when referring to a general beach experience.)

18. I can't believe that he did this.
(Correctoin:
2️⃣ "does" ❌ → "did" ✅
Since you are expressing disbelief about a past action, we use "did" (past simple).)

19. I always help my mother cook dinner.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "helping" ❌ → "help" ✅
"Always" is used with simple present ("help") because it's a regular habit.
2️⃣ "to cook" ❌ → "cook" ✅
After "help," we usually use the bare infinitive ("cook") instead of "to cook.")

20. Maybe this season will be very cold.

21. If you don't work hard, you'll fail.

22. He will tell me what to do.

23. I will try to spend more time with my friends.

24. I didn't know that he already arrived home.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "didn't knew" ❌ → "didn't know" ✅
After "didn't," always use the base form of the verb (know, not knew).
2️⃣ "he already arrived" ❌ → "he had already arrived" ✅
Since the action happened before another past moment, we use past perfect ("had arrived").
3️⃣ "at home" ❌ → "home" ✅
"Arrived home" is more natural than "arrived at home.")

25. By the time he arrived, I had already fallen asleep.
(Corrections:
1️⃣ "arrives" ❌ → "arrived" ✅
"By the time" refers to a past event, so we need past tense ("arrived").
2️⃣ "was already slept" ❌ → "had already fallen asleep" ✅
"Slept" is a past simple verb, but here we need past perfect ("had fallen asleep").)
