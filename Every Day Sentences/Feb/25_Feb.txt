(Corrections & Explanation:
"Despite it will raining" → "Despite the rain"
"Despite" is not followed by a full clause with a verb ("will raining" is incorrect).
Instead, use a noun: "Despite the rain" or "Despite it raining" (gerund form).
"Decided work" → "Planned work"
"Decided work" is not natural in English.
"Planned work" is a better collocation for tasks you have set for yourself.)
