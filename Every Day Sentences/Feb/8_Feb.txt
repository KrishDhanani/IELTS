"If you not want" → "If you don’t want"

The correct structure is "If you don’t want", not "If you not want."
"More sweet" → "Too much sweet"

"More sweet" is not the best phrasing here. "Too much sweet" sounds more natural.
Even better: "too much sugar" or "too many sweets" (depending on what you mean).
"Say directly" → "Say it directly"

"Say directly" is missing an object. We usually say "say it directly" to make it complete.)

"As heavy rain outside" → "As it is raining heavily outside"

"Heavy rain outside" is not a complete sentence.
The correct way is "it is raining heavily outside.")

"The waiter" instead of just "waiter"
