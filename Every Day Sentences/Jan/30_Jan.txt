13. If you speak the truth then everything will be all right.
("speaks" → corrected to "speak"
In conditional sentences, when the subject is "you", the verb should be in the base form, not with an "s."
❌ Incorrect: "If you speaks"
✅ Correct: "If you speak"
"truth" → corrected to "the truth"
We usually use "the" before "truth" when referring to something specific (e.g., "the truth" as a concept).
❌ Incorrect: "If you speak truth"
✅ Correct: "If you speak the truth")

❌ Incorrect: "He was studied"
